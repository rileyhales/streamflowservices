Global Streamflow Forecasting Project

The Global Streamflow Prediction Services were developed by Dr Michael Suffront and Dr Jim Nelson with their colleagues at Brigham Young University. This streamflow service provides a 15 day forecast of estimated streamflow and a simulation of flow since 1980 for every stream around the planet. This information is accessible through a rest API, a map interface for exploring the stream network and retrieving data, and an animated map showing the magnitude of flows across through each stream. (view the services section of the navigation)

The global hydrologic model is primarily driven by:

  1. A GIS watershed and stream network delineation using hydrologically conditioned DEMs courtesy of ESRI
  2. Medium-range (15 day) weather forecasts from the European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasting. The precipitation accumuation forecasts consist of a 51 member ensemble at .5 degree spatial resolution and 3-hour temporal resolution for 15 days and a .25 degree and 1-hourly forecast for 10 days.
  3. The rapid routing method that maps each of the 52 ensemble member's runoff estimation to a watershed and through the stream network.

This research has been supported by the European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasting (ECMWF), the Group on Earth Observations - Global Water Security (GEOGLOWS) and GEOSS Program, ESRI, the NASA SERVIR project, Microsoft Azure's AI for Earth Initiative, Brigham Young University and foundational work of other doctoral students, The World Bank, Tethys Platform, many other open source softwares and the online programming community, and the support of many people at all these institutions.
